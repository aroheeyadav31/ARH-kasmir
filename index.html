Culture

Kashmiri culture reflects a blend of Hindu, Muslim, and Buddhist traditions. People wear traditional *pheran*, a long woolen cloak, especially in winter. Handicrafts such as pashmina shawls and carpets are world-renowned. Music, poetry, and festivals like Eid and Navroz are celebrated with enthusiasm.

Nature

Kashmir is nestled in the Himalayas and known for its lush green valleys and snow-capped peaks. The scenic beauty of places like Gulmarg, Sonmarg, and Pahalgam attracts tourists throughout the year. Winter sports like skiing are popular during snowfall season.

Food

Kashmiri cuisine is rich, aromatic, and often cooked with saffron and dry fruits. Popular dishes include Rogan Josh (lamb curry), Yakhni (yogurt-based curry), and Dum Aloo. Wazwan is a traditional royal feast served during weddings with over 30 dishes. Kahwa and Noon Chai are famous Kashmiri teas.

Tourist Attraction

Dal Lake is the heart of Srinagar with beautiful houseboats and colorful shikaras (boats). Tourists can stay on houseboats and enjoy views of the Zabarwan Hills. Other attractions include Mughal Gardens, Shankaracharya Temple, and the floating vegetable market. Every season offers a unique charm in Kashmir.
